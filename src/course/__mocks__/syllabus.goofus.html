Course Description

Ecological Principles: Applications to Conservation is a comprehensive course that delves into the fundamental concepts of ecology and their relevance in addressing contemporary conservation challenges. Through a combination of theoretical instruction and practical applications, students will explore key ecological principles that help explain and mitigate the impacts of climate change, human activities, and invasive species on ecological systems. Through course activities, students will develop a solid foundation in the key principles of ecology and their direct applications to conservation. This knowledge will enable them to contribute effectively to the understanding and management of ecological systems and the preservation of biodiversity.

Course Outcomes

Students will be able to:

  1. Collect and interpret data related to biodiversity measures such as species richness, evenness, and diversity. 
  2. Apply fundamental concepts of ecology to contemporary conservation challenges.
  3. Explain the impact of climate change, human activities, and invasive species on ecological systems.
  4.  Interpret and evaluate scientific publications.
  5. Assess the validity and reliability of information sources pertaining to ecology and conservation.
  6. Apply the principles and practices of conservation ecology and wildlife management to promote biodiversity conservation.

Work will not be accepted if it is more than 6 days late.

No work will be accepted after the end of the term without an approved Incomplete request.

If you experience extenuating circumstances that prevent you from completing your work on time, reach out to your instructor as soon as possible.

The Unity Environmental University Honor Code

The Unity Environmental University Honor Code requires that students be honest in all academic work. By joining the Unity Environmental University community, students express their willingness to accept the responsibilities and privileges of the academic community. Furthermore, students understand that their name on any assignment—written or otherwise—shall be regarded as assurance that the work is the result of their own thought and study, except where quotation marks, references, footnotes, or other means of attribution acknowledge the use of other sources. Acknowledgment of collaboration shall be made in the work submitted. In examinations, students shall respond entirely on the basis of their own capacity without any assistance, except that authorized by the instructor.

Turnitin

Unity uses Turnitin as a plagiarism checker to support the academic integrity of its students. Turnitin is enabled for certain assignments throughout your program. You can tell if Turnitin is enabled for an assignment by the language at the bottom of the assignment submission page, which will require you to agree to the following: "I agree to the tool's End-User License Agreement. The work I submit is my original work, and the information has been correctly cited." You will not be able to submit until you agree.

For guidance on how Turnitin's Originality Reports will work, including how to use and what to expect from them, please watch this video of Turnitin from the student perspective. Also, reference their resources for Using Turnitin with Canvas as a Student.

Academic Dishonesty includes, but is not limited to—

Plagiarism

  • quoting, summarizing, or paraphrasing any part or all of a source without acknowledging the source in the text of any written work;
  • incorporating any information—data, statistics, examples, etc.— that is not common knowledge without attributing the source of that information;
  • using another person’s opinions, reasoning, or arguments; and
  • putting your name on an assignment someone else completed.

Cheating

  • claiming credit for work not done independently (excluding university support services such as the LRC) without giving credit for aid received; and
  • accepting any unauthorized aid or communication during examinations, and falsifying or deliberately misrepresenting data and/or submission of work.

Other Unacceptable Practice

  • submitting an assignment for one class in another class without approval of both instructors.

Any student found to be responsible for violating the Unity Environmental University Honor Code may be suspended or dismissed from the university.
